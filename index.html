About Us

About

A clock, a timeless invention, serves as a bridge between human lives and the inevitable march of time. It is an instrument designed to measure, keep, and display time, available in various forms such as analog, digital, pendulum, and even elaborate antique designs. The clock transcends mere functionality, embodying precision and order in a world filled with chaos. From waking us up each morning to tracking milestones throughout the day, it structures our lives, enabling us to balance work, leisure, and rest. Clocks also hold cultural and historical significance, showcasing craftsmanship and artistry through their intricate designs. Iconic landmarks like Big Ben or the Astronomical Clock in Prague stand as testaments to their enduring appeal. Beyond practicality, a clock symbolizes the fragile nature of time, urging us to appreciate its passage and live with purpose.

To maintain your watch's quality, keep it clean and avoid exposing it to extreme conditions. Service it regularly, store it safely, and handle it with care to ensure longevity and precision.

Whether you can swim or shower with your watch depends on its water resistance rating. Check the manual for details. Generally, watches with 50m resistance handle light splashes, 100m suits swimming, and above 200m works for diving. Avoid exposing non-waterproof watches to water.

To keep your watch in top condition, service it every 3-5 years or as recommended by the manufacturer. Regular maintenance ensures accuracy, prevents wear, and extends your watch's lifespan.

Yes, you can change your watch strap yourself with the right tools and a bit of care. Use a spring bar tool or small screwdriver to remove the old strap. Ensure compatibility with the new strap and handle the watch delicately to avoid damage. Follow manufacturer guidelines.

Watch battery life varies by type and usage. Quartz watches typically last 1-3 years, while smartwatches may need charging daily or weekly. Factors like functions used and battery quality affect longevity. Regular checks ensure your watch stays powered efficiently.
